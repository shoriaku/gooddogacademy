What Is A Service Dog?

A service dog helps a person with a disability lead a more independent life. The dogs are trained to do work and perform tasks for a person with a disability. The different tasks performed by the dog must be directly related to the person's disability.

What Do I Do As A Service Trainer?

I train dogs to do four types of service and assistance. The first service is guide dog training. This teaches dogs how to assist blind and visually impaired individuals navigate the environment. The second service is hearing. This teaches dogs how to alert deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals to important sounds. The third service is a general service training. This teaches dogs to work with individuals in wheelchairs, have autism, how to alert when someone has a seizure or other medical issues, etc. The fourth service is therapy training. This teaches dogs basic obedience and how to adapt to whatever environment that they are in.
